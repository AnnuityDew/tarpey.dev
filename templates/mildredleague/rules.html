Playoff Tiebreakers

I have a thing for the NFL tiebreakers, so we use a system similar to theirs. It's much more chaotic and interesting than a simple Points For tiebreaker, and I like how it puts strong emphasis on our division matchups (not to mention it has already paid off in the form of a few crazy playoff outcomes so far!).

Here's how it works:

  1. If two teams in the same division end up tied in overall record (i.e. both 7-7), tiebreakers will happen in this order:
    1. Head-to-head record
    2. Division record
    3. Points for
    4. Points against
    5. Coin toss (a real one, not random number generated)
  2. If two teams in different divisions end up tied, tiebreakers will happen in this order:
    1. Head-to-head record
    2. Points for
    3. Points against
    4. Coin toss (a real one, not random number generated)
  3. If 3+ teams end up tied, we just use the same tiebreakers described above depending on same division or different division. But there are two special rules that might apply:
    1. Ties are broken INSIDE each division first before comparing teams from different divisions.
    2. When PART of a 3+ way tie breaks (say one team breaks out but the other two teams are still tied), we go back to step 1 with just the teams that are left still tied.
  4. For a 3+ team tie that's all in the same division, head-to-head record just counts all games among teams in the tiebreaker. Some examples:
    • If three teams have played each other twice each (this is the case in all of our divisions), 3-1 beats 2-2 beats 1-3 would fully break the tie.
    • Or maybe everyone is 2-2...then we go to full division record of all three teams.
    • There's another case where one team escapes the tiebreaker, but others are still tied (in our three team example this could look something like 4-0, 1-3, 1-3). When this happens, the teams that are still tied start back at step 1 of the tiebreaker!
  5. For a three team tie across the three divisions, one of the teams has to have a SWEEP for head-to-head to apply. (This could be a winning sweep where they beat both of the other teams, or a losing sweep where they lost to both of the other teams.) If there's no sweep for anyone, it will go to Points For.
